About Me

I am a researcher with more than 15 years of experience advancing clean‑energy technologies, thermochemical conversion, catalysis, and process modeling. I hold a PhD in Chemical Engineering from École Polytechnique de Montréal and have spent the past decade as a Research Officer at the National Research Council of Canada, where I lead multidisciplinary R&D programs at the intersection of materials, energy, and industrial innovation.

My work focuses on developing advanced chemical processes and predictive modeling frameworks that bridge fundamental science with applied engineering. I have contributed to peer‑reviewed publications, patents, and collaborative research initiatives across academia, government, and industry. My research spans hydrogen production, CO₂ conversion, biomass valorization, and waste‑to‑energy technologies, with a strong emphasis on reactor development, kinetic modeling, and catalyst design.

I bring extensive experience in experimental design and advanced analytical techniques (GC‑MS, HPLC, TGA, BET, XRD, and more), along with a proven track record managing complex research programs, supervising teams, and coordinating multi‑institutional collaborations. My work increasingly integrates artificial intelligence to optimize processes and accelerate catalyst development.

Committed to safe and effective laboratory practice, I am skilled in hazard prevention, safety management, and regulatory compliance. I also enjoy teaching, mentoring, and sharing research outcomes through scientific presentations and conferences.
